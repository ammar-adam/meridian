# Investor demo film — Meridian

**Runtime:** ~3 minutes  
**Claim:** Meridian surfaces early Canadian companies from community sources (founders + domains + provenance) before US-centric tools index them — then briefs them against your fund thesis.

**Prod:** https://meridian-eight-sandy.vercel.app

---

## Before you hit record

1. Select your fund in the switcher (Panache if telling the Canada story).
2. Open Discover → paste a Canadian pre-seed thesis (or **Use fund mandate**) → **Run search** once and wait (~1–3 min cold). Refresh so the second open is cached.
3. Confirm the first screen is **Incubator · high** rows with **Founders:** and domains. Scroll only those rows on tape.
4. Start Brief on one domain company (e.g. scadable.com) so it is mid-flight or finished before the close.
5. Optional foil tab: https://getfundingfromavc.vercel.app (GiveFund = fabricated).
6. Do **not** open Team, show the Status panel, or claim EverTrace / PitchBook / national registry coverage.

---

## Beats

### 1. Open on Discover results (~25s)

> Early Canadian deal flow isn’t in Harmonic. It lives on Velocity, DMZ, CDL cohort pages — fragmented. Meridian structures it with founders, domains, and provenance.

Pan Incubator badges + Founders lines. Do not start on the landing page.

### 2. One company deep (~40s)

Click a fully enriched row (founders + domain). Spoken: cohort structure beats a name-search in StartupHub for these companies.

### 3. Foil cut (~25s) — optional

Cut to getfundingfromavc: fabricated portfolio company. Back to Meridian: provenance, not inventing from a fund marketing page.

### 4. Brief close (~45s)

Open the finished memo (or talk over the thesis-band drafting step). Point at the fund thesis band → Pursue.

### 5. Close (~15s)

> The memo is the delivery vehicle. The moat is community-sourced entities before they’re indexable.

Stop.

---

## Hard don’ts

- Scroll past thin / domain-null incubator stubs (they’re filtered on Canadian theses — still don’t invent stats)
- “We replace PitchBook / Harmonic”
- EverTrace live, national Corporations Canada, WHOIS rush
- Dead silence for a 60s Brief — talk over scrape/thesis-band progress or cut
